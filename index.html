Dire Inchini Afforestation Program

10-Year Transformation Overview (2015–2025)

Over the past decade, the Dire Inchini District has undergone a massive environmental transformation. By analyzing satellite imagery from 2015 to 2025, we have tracked significant improvements in overall plant health and vegetation cover across key local areas including Ajo Kersa, Buyama Roge, Rogge Daga, and Buyema Daballe.

Historically, these regions faced severe deforestation and soil erosion. However, community-led tree planting initiatives have drastically reversed this trend. Between 2015 and 2025, areas with poor plant health decreased by 40%, while thriving, healthy green spaces expanded by an incredible 300%.

This ecological recovery directly aligns with recent massive reforestation efforts. Between 2023 and 2025 alone, over 733,000 tree seedlings (including indigenous species like Olea africana and Juniper) were distributed and planted. Ajo Kersa and Buyama Roge showed the most dramatic vegetation gains due to these targeted interventions.
